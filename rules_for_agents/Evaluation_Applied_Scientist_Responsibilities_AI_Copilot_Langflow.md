## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Product Overview
The AI Copilot platform empowers business users to create and execute workflows from natural language inputs without the need for developer intervention. These workflows are automatically generated based on user-defined tasks, validated, and executed using Langflow. The platform generates results that need to be evaluated for quality, relevance, and trustworthiness.

As an **Evaluation / Applied Scientist**, your role is to design and implement the frameworks and methodologies for evaluating the results of these workflows. This includes assessing the semantic correctness, confidence scoring, and overall quality of the outputs, as well as providing users with actionable insights into the results.

Your work ensures that the platform delivers **high-quality, reliable, and explainable results**, allowing business users to trust the decisions made by the AI models and workflows.

---

## 2. Key Responsibilities of the Evaluation / Applied Scientist

### 2.1 Result Evaluation Framework
Your primary responsibility will be to design a **framework** that evaluates the quality and relevance of the results generated by the platform. This includes:
- Developing models that evaluate the **semantic correctness** of the generated workflows and their outputs.
- Designing confidence scoring mechanisms that reflect the **reliability** of the results.
- Implementing a **quality evaluation system** that helps users understand the limitations and reliability of the results.

#### Deliverables:
- **Result evaluation model** for assessing the accuracy and relevance of outputs.
- **Confidence scoring system** to communicate the reliability of results.
- **Quality evaluation framework** to highlight potential issues in outputs.

### 2.2 Semantic Fit and Relevance Evaluation
You will ensure that the generated results meet the expectations of the business user. This includes:
- Evaluating whether the results are **semantically correct** and aligned with the user’s task.
- Designing evaluation methods that assess the **relevance** of results, ensuring that they align with the user’s original request.
- Identifying discrepancies and gaps between the task specification and the results, and flagging them for further review.

#### Deliverables:
- **Semantic correctness model** to check if results align with user expectations.
- **Relevance scoring system** to assess how well the outputs match the input task.
- **Discrepancy detection system** to identify and flag mismatches between input and output.

### 2.3 Confidence Scoring and Trust Metrics
To help users trust the results provided by the platform, you will:
- Design a **confidence scoring system** that quantifies the level of certainty or trustworthiness in the results.
- Develop **trust metrics** to communicate to the user how confident the system is in the results, and explain why certain results may be more reliable than others.
- Ensure that the confidence score reflects the **reliability** of the task execution, the data quality, and the workflow model.

#### Deliverables:
- **Confidence scoring model** for each result produced by the system.
- **Trust metrics** to help users assess the quality of results.
- **Explanation framework** to clarify why the confidence score is given for each result.

### 2.4 Explainability and Transparency
Your work will involve ensuring that the platform is **explainable** and transparent. This includes:
- Providing users with clear explanations for the results, especially when the confidence is low or the output is uncertain.
- Designing systems that offer **rationales** for why specific results were generated based on the inputs provided.
- Creating **traceability mechanisms** to link the output back to the original task and input data.

#### Deliverables:
- **Explainability framework** that provides reasons behind the results.
- **Transparency mechanisms** that help users understand how results were derived.
- **Traceability system** that allows users to track the workflow from input to output.

### 2.5 Continuous Model Improvement and Feedback Loop
To ensure the system remains accurate and effective, you will:
- Work with the **AI Architect** to define how evaluation results can feed back into the improvement of the AI models used for task understanding, clarification, and workflow planning.
- Develop strategies for **continuous evaluation** of results to identify areas for model improvement over time.
- Ensure that the evaluation system can accommodate new data and adapt to changing business requirements.

#### Deliverables:
- **Continuous improvement plan** for model evaluation and feedback loops.
- **Evaluation pipeline** that updates models based on feedback and new data.
- **Feedback mechanisms** for integrating user evaluations into the model training process.

### 2.6 Collaboration with Cross-functional Teams
As an **Evaluation / Applied Scientist**, you will work closely with other teams to ensure that the evaluation framework integrates well with the overall platform:
- **AI Architect**: To ensure that the evaluation models are aligned with AI-driven workflows.
- **Product Manager**: To ensure that evaluation metrics meet business objectives and provide actionable insights to users.
- **Backend Engineers**: To ensure that the evaluation system is seamlessly integrated into the platform and scales with user demand.
- **UX/Product Designer**: To ensure that the evaluation results are presented in an understandable and user-friendly manner.

#### Deliverables:
- **Cross-functional collaboration** with AI, product, and backend teams to integrate evaluation processes.
- **Design reviews** with UX teams to ensure that evaluation results are clearly presented to users.
- **Testing and validation** of the evaluation models with product and AI teams.

## 4. Success Metrics for Evaluation / Applied Scientist

### 4.1 Evaluation Quality Metrics
- **Semantic correctness**: The percentage of outputs that are semantically correct according to the task specification.
- **Relevance**: The proportion of outputs that match the user’s original task and intent.
- **Discrepancy rate**: The rate at which output discrepancies or mismatches are detected and flagged.

### 4.2 Confidence Scoring Metrics
- **Confidence accuracy**: The correlation between the system’s confidence score and the actual reliability of the results.
- **Trust metric reliability**: The reliability of the trust metrics in predicting the quality of the results.
- **User feedback**: User satisfaction with the explanations provided for the confidence score and results.

### 4.3 System Performance Metrics
- **Evaluation speed**: The time it takes to evaluate and score a task’s results.
- **Scalability**: The system’s ability to handle increasing task volumes and complexity.
- **User trust**: How confident users feel in the platform's results, as measured by feedback surveys and NPS.

## 6. PRD Alignment

### 6.1 Workstream ownership
- **Workstream D — Validation & Trust** (owner; contributors: AI Architect, Domain Expert, PM) — PRD §25.3.
- Contributor to Workstream C (Agent/Planner/Evaluator Design).

### 6.2 Four levels of correctness (PRD §18.2) — all are yours
You are responsible for defining methodology and instrumentation for **all four levels**:

1. **Structural correctness** — graph validity, required inputs/parameters, input/output contract compatibility, component-combination admissibility.
2. **Execution correctness** — ability to execute, sample-run success, runtime stability, retry/timeout behavior, output schema validity.
3. **Semantic correctness** — workflow actually solves the user's task; requested dimensions/outputs are present (e.g., "trends" means trends, not generic summary).
4. **Business correctness** — result is usable for the business question; conclusions are data-backed; confidence present; limitations surfaced; human review assigned when needed.

### 6.3 Required validation stages (PRD §18.3)
Seven stages must be implemented and pass in order:
1. Task understanding validation.
2. Spec sanity validation.
3. Workflow structural validation.
4. Data readiness validation (consumes Data Engineer's readiness report).
5. Sample-run validation.
6. Semantic result validation.
7. Final report confidence gate.

### 6.4 Evidence model (PRD §18.4)
Every significant output carries, where applicable:
- evidence examples,
- counts or coverage,
- source mapping,
- confidence signal,
- limitation note.

Design evidence schema and enforce it in Report Generator output.

### 6.5 Handling low-confidence outputs (PRD §18.5)
When confidence falls below the threshold (per task spec's `confidence_threshold` — Appendix A):
- Do not mask.
- Show cause: sparse data, noisy data, conflicting evidence, unstable clustering, weak task linkage.
- Offer actions: refine task, improve source, adjust analysis, human review, narrow scope.

### 6.6 Trust metrics to instrument (PRD §22.4)
- Evidence coverage rate.
- Human agreement score.
- Low-confidence rate.
- Override rate by expert reviewers.
- Abuse detection precision/recall (co-owned with Security/AI Architect).
- False-positive block rate.

### 6.7 Quality benchmarks
Maintain labeled benchmarks per supported use case (PRD §10) covering semantic fit, evidence quality, and business usefulness (with Domain Expert).

- **Security / Privacy Engineer**: Co-instrument abuse detection precision/recall.
