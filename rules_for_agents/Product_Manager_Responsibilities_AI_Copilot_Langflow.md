## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Product Overview
This AI Copilot platform will enable business users to autonomously create workflows for solving business problems using natural language, without requiring developer involvement. The platform will provide users with the ability to:
- Formulate a task in natural language.
- Get clarified on missing information through a guided setup.
- Automatically generate workflows.
- Validate and run these workflows.
- Obtain actionable reports, insights, and results.

This platform will leverage Langflow as a runtime orchestration layer but add a control plane for task understanding, clarification, planning, validation, and governance. The platform will handle data security, trust, and correct output, giving business users confidence in the results.

### 1.2 Problem and Solution
**Problem:** Business users need to validate hypotheses and generate reports but often rely on developers or data scientists to create workflows and analyze data. This dependency causes delays and inefficiencies.
**Solution:** The AI Copilot platform will provide a self-serve workflow generation system that:
- Supports natural language input.
- Automatically builds and validates workflows.
- Provides explainable results and allows business users to act on insights quickly.

## 2. Product Vision

The vision for this product is to enable **self-serve** workflow generation for business users without relying on developers. The goal is to create a platform where users can input their tasks, get the workflows automatically generated, and ensure that these workflows solve their problems reliably and efficiently.

## 3. Key Responsibilities of the Product Manager

### 3.1 Product Vision Ownership
As the Product Manager, you will be responsible for owning the **vision** of the product. This includes clearly articulating the overall value of the product to all stakeholders and ensuring that the team remains aligned with business objectives.

### 3.2 Roadmap Management
You will be responsible for defining and managing the product **roadmap**. This involves:
- Identifying key milestones.
- Setting priorities based on business needs.
- Managing timelines for delivery of features.
- Ensuring that the platform evolves in alignment with business goals.

### 3.3 Stakeholder Communication
As PM, you will be the key **communication channel** between:
- Business users (internal stakeholders, business teams) and the development team.
- The product and the executive team.
- Business leads and tech leads to ensure requirements are understood and delivered.

You will also handle **product updates** and track how changes are being implemented according to expectations.

### 3.4 User and Market Research
The PM is responsible for ensuring that the product **meets market needs** and **solves real user problems**. You will need to:
- Lead user research, personas development, and JTBD (Jobs to Be Done) analysis.
- Track user feedback and iterate on product features based on insights.
- Monitor market trends to ensure competitive advantage and relevance.

### 3.5 Feature Prioritization
You will make data-driven decisions on which **features** to prioritize. This includes balancing user needs with technical feasibility, focusing on **high-impact features** that will drive the most value for users. This also includes defining product requirements, user stories, and acceptance criteria for new features.

## 4. Detailed Tasks and Deliverables for Product Manager

### 4.1 Define Product Vision and Strategy
The PM must create and maintain the product **vision** that aligns with the business needs. This should clearly define:
- The problem being solved.
- The target audience.
- The key features and value propositions of the platform.
- Why now is the right time to build this product.

#### Deliverables:
- 1–2 page product vision statement.
- Strategy brief for the platform.
- Alignment with business goals.

### 4.2 Own the Product Roadmap
The PM will define and manage the product **roadmap**, ensuring alignment with the broader business strategy and ensuring timely execution. Prioritization must be based on value to the business and technical feasibility.

#### Deliverables:
- Product roadmap with prioritized features and timelines.
- Key milestones (e.g., MVP release, early access, pilot).
- Alignment between business needs and development timelines.

### 4.3 Drive Cross-functional Collaboration
The PM will ensure close collaboration between **engineering**, **design**, **security**, and **business teams** to deliver a fully integrated product that meets both business and technical needs.

#### Deliverables:
- Collaboration model between engineering, design, and business stakeholders.
- Regular meetings to ensure alignment and remove roadblocks.
- Cross-functional communication strategy for product updates and feedback loops.

### 4.4 User Research and Requirements Gathering
The PM is responsible for deeply understanding the needs of the **end-users** (business analysts, CX leads, etc.). This includes:
- Gathering requirements through direct user interviews.
- Identifying **user pain points** and defining solutions.
- Translating these insights into **user stories** and **acceptance criteria**.

#### Deliverables:
- Personas and Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) map.
- User story backlog.
- Feature prioritization based on business impact.

### 4.5 Monitor Product Success Metrics
The PM will define **success metrics** for the platform. These should include:
- **Product usage metrics**: Adoption rates, engagement, retention.
- **Business metrics**: Time-to-insight, user satisfaction, revenue generation.
- **Operational metrics**: Platform uptime, incident response times.

The PM will also lead the team in making adjustments based on these metrics to improve the product post-launch.

#### Deliverables:
- Success metrics and KPIs.
- Continuous monitoring and reporting on product success.
- Data analysis to inform future features.

### 4.6 Define and Manage Product Backlog
The PM is responsible for creating and managing the **product backlog**. This involves:
- Writing clear user stories.
- Organizing user stories into sprints.
- Ensuring clear acceptance criteria are established for each story.
- Collaborating with the engineering team to ensure that the backlog reflects the correct technical requirements.

#### Deliverables:
- Clear and prioritized backlog of user stories.
- Acceptance criteria for each user story.
- Regular backlog grooming sessions with the development team.

## 5. Key Product Milestones

### 5.1 Milestone 1: Initial Product Vision and Stakeholder Alignment
- Define and document the product vision.
- Gather feedback from key stakeholders to ensure alignment with business goals.
- Present the vision to the executive team for buy-in.

### 5.2 Milestone 2: Roadmap Definition and Feature Prioritization
- Develop and prioritize the product roadmap.
- Align the roadmap with business goals.
- Present the roadmap to stakeholders for approval.

### 5.3 Milestone 3: UX and Feature Design Finalization
- Work closely with the UX team to finalize the user journey and design mockups.
- Ensure all core features are scoped out and detailed for development.

### 5.4 Milestone 4: MVP Launch and Feedback Collection
- Oversee the development and release of the MVP.
- Work with business teams to gather user feedback on the MVP.
- Iterate on the product based on user feedback.

### 5.5 Milestone 5: Full Launch and Product Scaling
- Prepare for the broader product launch.
- Focus on scaling the platform, onboarding more users, and integrating with other systems.
- Measure product success against defined KPIs and adjust as needed.

- Collaborate on defining access control, user roles, and compliance requirements.

- Present product updates and gather feedback for further iterations.

## 7. Success Metrics and KPIs

### 7.1 Product Metrics
- **User engagement**: Adoption rate, active users, retention rate.
- **Time-to-insight**: How quickly business users can get actionable insights.
- **Self-serve completion rate**: Percentage of tasks completed without developer involvement.

### 7.2 Business Metrics
- **Customer satisfaction (CSAT)**: User feedback on usability and usefulness.
- **Conversion from request to executed workflow**: Efficiency of the platform in converting a business problem into an executable workflow.

### 7.3 Operational Metrics
- **Platform stability**: Uptime and incident response.
- **Feature delivery success**: Timeliness of feature delivery and meeting business requirements.
